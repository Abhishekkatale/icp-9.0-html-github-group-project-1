Hostel Info and Admission

A hostel is a form of low-cost, short-term shared sociable lodging where guests can rent a bed, usually a bunk bed in a dormitory sleeping 4â€“20 people, with shared use of a lounge and usually a kitchen. Rooms can be mixed or single and have private or shared bathrooms. Private rooms may also be available. Hostels are popular forms of lodging for backpackers,however very few impose age limits, so hostels are an option for travellers of all ages and styles. The benefits of hostels include lower costs and opportunities to meet people from different places, find travel partners, and share travel experiences. Some hostels, such as in India or Hostelling International, cater to a niche market of travelers. Different hostels can be known for offering different experiences. For example, one hostel might feature in-house social gatherings such as movie nights or communal dinners, another might feature local tours, one might be known for its parties.

( Hostel Facilities )

  1. Clean and Comfortable Rooms: Both dormitory-style and private rooms should be well-maintained, clean, and comfortable.
  2. Free Wi-Fi: Reliable and fast internet access throughout the hostel.
  3. Communal Kitchen: A well-equipped kitchen where guests can prepare their meals.
  4. Common Areas: Spaces for socializing, such as lounges, game rooms, or outdoor terraces.
  5. Secure Storage: Lockers or storage areas for guests to securely store their belongings.



Food Timing Fees

The menu usually includes a mix of breakfast, lunch, and dinner, often served in a communal dining hall. Breakfast might feature items such as bread, eggs, cereals, and fruits, while lunch and dinner commonly offer rice, chapati, curries, vegetables, and sometimes meat or fish.
Typically, hostels enforce a curfew, requiring residents to return by a specific time in the evening, often around 9 or 10 PM. This helps ensure that everyone is accounted for and reduces the risk of late-night incidents. Hostel gates usually open early in the morning, around 6 or 7 AM, allowing residents to start their day.
The fee structure of a hostel typically(65 k to 1 lakh /year) includes several components, reflecting the various services and amenities provided to residents. At the core, there is the accommodation fee, which varies depending on the type of room selected, such as single, double, or dormitory-style rooms.

Hostel admission typically involves a systematic process to ensure that students secure accommodation. Initially, interested students must fill out an application form, either online or offline, providing necessary personal and academic details. This is followed by the submission of relevant documents, such as proof of admission, identity proof, and photographs. Some hostels might require an entrance fee or a security deposit. The allocation of rooms is usually based on availability, with preferences given to criteria such as year of study or academic merit. Once allocated, students are expected to adhere to the hostel rules and regulations, which are designed to maintain a conducive living and study environment. Regular inspections and meetings may be conducted to address any issues and ensure the smooth functioning of the hostel facilities.
